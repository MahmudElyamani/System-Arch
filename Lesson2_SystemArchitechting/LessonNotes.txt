Moore vs Meaky state machines:
• A moore machine performs actions when entering a state, for example a led would turn on when its time comes.
• A mealy machine performs actions when transitioning from a state, for example a led wouldn't turn on unless its time comes and a button is pressed.

A MCU system consists of basic layers, for example:
• The MCU communicates with the I2C that's tied to the ultrasonic sensor
• It also can have a timer that produces pwm signal to drive a motor.
• each of these signals is passed on through a driver for each, DC motor and ultrasonic sensor.
• above them is whats called Algorithm which contains the states
• this is called HAL (hardware abstraction layer)

Sytem architecting / Design sequence: (37:04)
Assuming you're the business owner communicating with a customer, here's how you should lay out your plan of operation,
• First step is to meet with the customer and deduce a case study out of his requirements, which is basically a list of operations
  that are supposed to be performed followed by assumption by yourself of what you have and don't have in terms of information.
• Choose the method of operation you want, including when to do meetings and how to organize teams.
• Get into the requirements that needs to be met and calculate the price for each requirement, then speak with the customer 
  to verify these requirements and to see if he needs any changes to the specs. This also helps getting you out of jobs
  that are not meant to be performed by you in case you're working as a freelancer. Eng.Keroles suggested we use UML2.
• next is the space exploration/partitioning which includes determining the MCU, how many will be used, how will the operation be
  split between them, etc.. some big companies use tools that are specifically designed to compat this matt
  er but in lower-scale
  projects you will only need to know what MCU you will be using based on the requirements.
• System analysis is a step that can be performed earlier than this point, but its basically grouping the system requirements into
  three UML diagrams which is yours to see just to start implementing
• next is the designing and system testing and implementation which we'll be going over in the 2nd term.
